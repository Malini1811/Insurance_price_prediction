Terms and Conditions for Your Insurance Prediction

Important features to consider while predicting your insurance cost:

  1. Age: Insurance premiums often increase with age due to higher health risks associated with aging. Younger individuals typically have lower premiums compared to older individuals.
  2. Gender: In some insurance models, gender can influence premiums. For example, females may have different premium rates compared to males based on actuarial data related to health risks.
  3. BMI (Body Mass Index): BMI is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. Higher BMI values may indicate higher health risks and could lead to higher insurance premiums.
  4. Number of Children: Insurance costs can be affected by the number of dependents (such as children) covered under the policy. More dependents may result in higher premiums.
  5. Smoking Status: Smokers generally have higher insurance premiums due to increased health risks associated with smoking.
  6. Region: The geographical area where the insured person lives can impact insurance costs. Different regions may have varying healthcare costs and risk factors.
